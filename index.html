Our AI Models

Brain tumors are abnormal growths of cells in the brain that can be either harmless or cancerous. They can lead to serious health complications, such as increased intracranial pressure, seizures, neurological issues, and can be fatal if left untreated depending on their size, location, and type. This scan will require an image of a brain MRI.

Breast cancer is a type of cancer that begins in the cells of the breast, often presenting as a lump or change in breast tissue. It can spread to other parts of the body if not detected early, leading to serious health complications, but with early detection and treatment, the prognosis is often more favorable. This scan will require an image of a breast MRI.

Lung cancer is a type of cancer that begins in the lungs, often caused by smoking or exposure to harmful substances. It can lead to symptoms like persistent cough, difficulty breathing, chest pain, and fatigue, and can spread to other parts of the body. This can make it a potentially life-threatening disease if not diagnosed and treated early. This scan will require an image of a lung MRI.

Bone Cancer is a type of cancer that begins in the cells of the bone, often causing pain, swelling, or fractures. It can spread to other parts of the body if not treated early, leading to serious complications, but with early diagnosis and treatments like surgery, chemotherapy, or radiation, the prognosis can improve. This scan will require an image of a bone MRI.

Pneumonia is an infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs, often caused by bacteria, viruses, or fungi. It can lead to symptoms like cough, fever, difficulty breathing, chest pain, and fatigue. In severe cases, pneumonia can cause complications such as respiratory failure or sepsis, making it a potentially life-threatening condition if not diagnosed and treated early.This scan will require an image of a lung x-ray.

Dementia is a neurological disorder that affects memory, thinking, and daily functioning, often caused by diseases like Alzheimer's. It can lead to symptoms such as confusion, difficulty with communication, mood changes, and loss of independence. As the condition progresses, it can severely impact quality of life and require full-time care. This scan will require an image of a brain MRI.

Kidney cancer is a type of cancer that begins in the kidneys, often linked to risk factors like smoking, obesity, and high blood pressure. It can cause symptoms such as blood in the urine, lower back pain, fatigue, and unexplained weight loss, and may spread to other parts of the body. If not detected and treated early, it can become life-threatening. This scan will require an image of a kidney MRI.

Covid 19 is a viral infection caused by SARS-CoV-2, primarily affecting the respiratory system. Symptoms range from mild (fever, cough) to severe (pneumonia, respiratory failure). It spreads through respiratory droplets and can lead to complications like organ damage. Early diagnosis and treatments, including antivirals, oxygen therapy, and supportive care, improve outcomes. This scan will require a CT scan of a lung.

Heart Disease is a broad term for conditions affecting the heart, including coronary artery disease, heart failure, and arrhythmias. Symptoms may include chest pain, shortness of breath, and fatigue. It can lead to severe complications like heart attacks or stroke. Risk factors include high blood pressure, cholesterol, and lifestyle habits. Diagnosis involves ECG, echocardiograms, or angiography.

Prostate Cancer is a malignancy in the prostate gland, often growing slowly but sometimes aggressively spreading. Symptoms may include difficulty urinating, blood in urine, or pelvic pain. Risk factors include age, genetics, and diet. Diagnosis typically requires a PSA blood test, biopsy, or MRI.

Multiple Schlerosis is a chronic autoimmune disease that damages the protective covering of nerves in the brain and spinal cord. Symptoms vary but may include vision problems, muscle weakness, and coordination issues. It can lead to disability over time. Diagnosis involves MRI, spinal fluid analysis, and neurological exams.

Diabetes is a metabolic disorder characterized by high blood sugar levels due to insulin resistance (Type 2) or inadequate insulin production (Type 1). Symptoms include excessive thirst, frequent urination, and fatigue. Long-term complications include nerve damage, kidney disease, and cardiovascular issues. Diagnosis is through blood tests like fasting glucose and A1C.
