Brain tumors are abnormal growths of cells in the brain that can be either harmless or cancerous. They can lead to serious health complications, such as increased intracranial pressure, seizures, neurological issues, and can be fatal if left untreated depending on their size, location, and type.

Breast cancer is a type of cancer that begins in the cells of the breast, often presenting as a lump or change in breast tissue. It can spread to other parts of the body if not detected early, leading to serious health complications, but with early detection and treatment, the prognosis is often more favorable.

Lung cancer is a type of cancer that begins in the lungs, often caused by smoking or exposure to harmful substances. It can lead to symptoms like persistent cough, difficulty breathing, chest pain, and fatigue, and can spread to other parts of the body. This can make it a potentially life-threatening disease if not diagnosed and treated early.
